How are these numbers calculated?

Media and graphics hours come from ODL's own per-deliverable estimation table (e.g., a studio lecture ≈ 8.5h, a 3-minute explainer animation ≈ 100h), summed across the deliverables typical for a project of this type and size — and validated against our logged time (observed median ≈ 12.8 h per finished short video, n=5 fully-tracked exemplars). Project-management hours are calibrated from ODL's actual logged PM time: 0.6–1.6 h per calendar week (middle 50%, 14 PM-tracked projects, 679 tracked PM hours). The "similar real projects" line comes from 2,053 logged staff hours across 55 projects (25 fully tracked), e.g. full courses: median 43 staff-hours (excl. PM) and a median 31-week timeline (middle half 18–42 wk, n=23). Design and faculty-time figures are ODL planning estimates (we have not yet logged faculty time) and are shown as ranges, not fixed numbers — they tighten as we track more projects. The Track Record tab draws on ODL's Impact Tracker in Asana: project status plus outcome fields like assets delivered and faculty satisfaction. Asset counts feed back into the estimate (the "second opinion" line) only once enough fully tracked projects match the tracker. These are estimates for planning, not commitments.

These are planning estimates, not commitments. Every project is unique. An ODL team member will confirm scope, timeline, and effort with you during your consultation, and any formal commitments are set in your Project Charter and approved by the ODL Director.
